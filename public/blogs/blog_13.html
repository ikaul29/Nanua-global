Power of a Handshake

You might have heard that you only have seven seconds to make a good first impression. Much of that impression is made up of your appearance, your perceived confidence and competence, and your friendliness. What you might not know is that a good handshake can radically boost the first impression you make, and that it can help you build rapport equivalent to that of a three hour meeting! The way you shake hands speaks volumes about who you are as a person. For some people a handshake is just a useless formality but to others it is a massive indication of a person’s depth of character, trustworthiness and strength. Your handshake conveys so much more than an initial greeting. Ancient history tells us that the shaking of hands was a gesture of peace, a sign that you hold no weapons. In sports after the game is finished, the teams or opponents shake hands, for a sign of good sportsmanship and respect .In business, regardless if the meeting or gathering was a success or not, people shake hands at the beginning and end of the encounter. A weak handshake lacks character, no eye contact, and the person gives you their hand like a limp wet towel. A weak handshake is a sign of weakness, lack of interest and nervousness. A real handshake shows true grit and comes with direct eye contact to let them know you are here; you are alive and breathing, and you are ready for anything. When introducing yourself, make sure you straighten your posture, have a clear voice, look them dead in the eye, and shake their hand like you mean it. In that moment you show personality, confidence and camaraderie. Too strong a handshake might imply too much dominance and is often considered to be rude. A few tips on how you can make the perfect handshake:

  • Look the person in the eyes

Always make sure you look a person in the eyes when you shake their hand, no matter how busy or brief the handshake might be.

  • Use a firm grip

A firm grip, shows confidence, strength and enthusiasm, be careful not to go overboard.

  • Don’t be too hasty

A handshake should be inviting but not rushed. Make sure you don’t overdo the shaking.

  • Make correct use of the left hand

Give a firm shake with the right hand and you may use the left hand to touch them on the shoulder or elbow region, but sometimes the “correct” use of the left hand is not to use it at all. Use your own intelligence to determine the best use of the left hand. Therefore, handshake is extremely important to our society. Peace treaties have been promised over a handshake. Wars have been ended by a handshake. Great business deals have begun because of a good handshake. A good handshake is a first impression that will last a long time in person’s mind. A good handshake is a strong trait that many lack in important times. It is upto you to decide whether you want to leave a good impression for the next hundred people you meet. Do you want them to be impressed by your handshake and respect you or simply forget you.
