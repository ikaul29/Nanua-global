Importance of Soft Skills in changing times

The importance of soft skills is coming to light, now 92% of recruiters believe that soft skills are as significant as hard skills or perhaps more. With the rising excitement about soft skills read on to learn more about soft skills and their importance.

What are soft skills?

Soft skills are a mix of social skills and interpersonal skills that enable a person to interact effectively. These are the interpersonal skills that everyone should have. The importance of soft skills has increased tremendously in this competitive world. They even provide you an edge over other people during job recruitment. Speaking of which, these skills are also set to be the most in-demand skills in the future by the World Economic Forum’s report on Future of Jobs. While it is not easy to define and analyze soft skills, most of these skills include communication, confidence, and listening. A person picks up soft skills from his day-to-day life but it becomes an important tool when one is a student or a working professional. They build your relationship with other people. They enable you to work in groups and take personal responsibility for your work.

Significance of soft skills

Soft skills are underrated, and there is no focused training provided for them like for hard skills such as coding and other physical skills. They are crucial to interact with the clients and work with the team members. It is important so the quality of work and fruitfulness are maintained. They have less to do with knowledge or training and more to do with personality traits. The base of a successful career may be laid by knowledge and training, but they are only boosted by soft skills. While technical skills may get someone’s foot at the door of opportunities, his people skills will open most of the doors to come. Work ethic, attitude, communication skills, emotional intelligence, and a whole host of other personal attributes are the skills that ensure holistic development.

One of the major reasons to learn these soft skills is that despite digitization and automation, there are still some ‘human’ skills or emotions that robots cannot perform or own. So, in the upcoming era when people will be losing their jobs due to AI and robots, there are still chances for the employees to survive this or to get a new job due to these soft skills. Alexandra Levit, author of “Humanity Works,” wrote an article that “In a business climate dominated by human/machine collaboration, “the skills that make us more human are more important than ever”
