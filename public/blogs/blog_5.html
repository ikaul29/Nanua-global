How to improve verbal communication skills?

What is verbal communication?

Verbal communication is the use of words, and sounds to share information with other people which includes both spoken and written communication. Speaker give words to feelings, thoughts, ideas and opinions and expresses them in form of speeches, discussions, and conversations.

Verbal communication requires both a speaker to transmit the message, and a listener to make sense of the message.

Key elements of verbal communication

  • Tone of speaker
  • Vocabulary
  • Speed and volume of voice
  • Grammar
  • Quality of words

  • Choose your words wisely: -The choice of words and vocabulary should be spot on for the speaker. Think about the perspective of the audience: - Try to think about how someone else will understand what you are trying to communicate, particularly if they have less knowledge about your topic.
  • Proper Tone: -Use your tone in a proper manner as it gives the picture of your true feelings and attitude. Use voice inflection to add emphasis to important points.
  • Reflecting and Clarifying: - Reflecting often involves paraphrasing the message communicated to you by the speaker in your own words.
  • Be concise and clear: - Do not add irrelevant elements in your speech. Get to the point so that your listener can understand better and clear.
  • Be considerate: - Always include consideration for your audience when planning out a speech. Practice in the mirror: -It can help a lot in improving verbal skills as you can pinpoint your mistakes and judge yourself.
  • Make some mental notes: - Before you start a conversation make some mental notes on what information you want to give to your audience and also decide the best way of conveying it to the recipient.
  • Be patient: - Persistent efforts, consistency, and patience can help a person achieve their goals.
