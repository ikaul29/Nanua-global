How to improve non-verbal communication skills?

What is non-verbal communication?

Nonverbal communication means communicating to the recipient using gestures, facial expressions, tone of voice, eye contact, body language, body posture, and other ways without using language.

Prominent points of non-verbal communication

  • Boosts confidence.
  • Creates positive impression.
  • Makes you look professional.
  • Non-verbal cues are more meaningful than the actual words.
  • Establish meaningful interactions in everyday life.

Key elements of non-verbal communication

  • Environment: -It is an integral part of the dynamic communication process as the perception of one’s environment influences one’s reaction to it.
  • Touch: -Handshakes can help establish trust before you take the stage. Body movements: - Body movements replace verbal messages as it complements the verbal message by reinforcing the main idea.
  • Eye contact: -It shows the level of confidence and trust in the eyes of the speaker. Physical characteristics: -It includes nodding, hand gestures, sweating while being nervous.

12 Ways to improve non-verbal communication skills

  • Monitor your facial expressions: -It is a most important thing as it can help a lot in improving one’s non-verbal skills.
  • Different behaviour: -Do not give fake expressions while speaking to your audience. Be confident: -A confident person stands out easily from the rest of the group. Ask questions: -Take help and ask questions to your trainers or friends on how you can improve your non-verbal skills.
  • Pay attention to proximity: - Different cultures view proximity in various ways, so learn to read the body language of others.
  • Work on your body postures: -Posture conveys a lot of things about the person’s communication pattern.
  • Avoid slouching: -Sit with your back straight up against the chair or lean slightly forward to convey engagement.
  • Eliminate fidgeting: -Avoid fidgeting and shaking of limbs.
  • Be a patient Listener: -Listen carefully to others and do not interrupt them in between their speech.
  • Smile: -Smiling appropriately makes your listener feel comfortable.
  • Maintain open arms: -It is important as folded arms can convey defensiveness.
  • Eye contact: - It is an important aspect of communication as it conveys that the person is confident while speaking.
